# Introduction to Interactive Design 
>## Course Description (Paragraph 1):
Introduction to Interactive Design is a foundational course (often catalogued under DCOM 3002) that immerses students in the core concepts and practices of interactive design. The course builds on students’ beginning skills in visual communication and web technologies, then broadens into key areas such as storytelling, usability, accessibility, user interface (UI) design, user experience (UX) research, and information architecture. It integrates both theoretical understanding and practical application, encouraging students to experiment, create, and collaborate using both code and visual media. Through project‑driven work, students develop the conceptual and technical competencies that support further study or entry‑level professional practice in digital and interactive media fields.

>## Course Focus & Value (Paragraph 2):
The learning experience in Introduction to Interactive Design emphasizes a balance of creative thinking and digital execution. By studying how interactive systems communicate with users and how design choices impact the effectiveness of digital products, students gain a strong foundation in crafting meaningful interactions. The course’s hands‑on projects and design explorations help students apply principles of usability and aesthetics to real‑world challenges, laying the groundwork for future work in UX/UI design, web and app interfaces, and other roles in the interactive media industry.
